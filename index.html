CrySiV, a MatLab function

The CrySiV, acronym of Crystallization Simulation and Visualization Tool, is a MatLab based function for simulation of cooling crystallization processes. The CrySiV contains primary and secondary nucleation, growth and dissolution as crystallization mechanisms and enables the simulation of crystallization of 1D and 2D crystals. The CrySiV applies high resolution finite volume method for PBE solution, implemented in the form of compiled C programs. CrySiV enables optional GPU acceleration on nVidia CUDA capable units. The function has similar calling syntax to the MatLab’s inner functions and during the development high accent was put on the efficient implementation; both for the sake of serving the needs of process engineers.

  • Name: CrySiV Function
  • Recent version: 1.1
  • Developed for: Crystallization optimization and control

CrySiV, MatLab GUI

The CrySiV MatLab Graphical User Interface (GUI) is an extension of the CrySiV function. Is based on the solvers of CrySiV function but it has an interactive and easy to use user interface. The GUI eases considerably the parameterization and ensures interactive communication between the solver and user. The simulation results can be saved, re-played (from the saved file), figures and animated .gif files can be generated. Moreover, it offers the possibility of comparison of simulation results. These features are all designed to help to understand deeper the crystallizing systems. Despite of the fact that the highly optimized solvers are used, the GUI operation has significant computational burden thus the MatLab based CrySiV GUI is more attractive but much slower than the CrySiV function.

  • Name: CrySiV MatLab GUI
  • Recent version: 1.1
  • Developed for: Interactive crystallization simulation

CrySiV, Standalone GUI

The standalone CrySiV GUI application, frum functional point of view, is identical with the CrySiV MatLab GUI. The major difference is that it not requires the MatLab but it needs to be installed. In contrast with the MatLab GUI the standalone GUI is completely compiled (not only the solvers). For this reason it runs considerably faster. During the instalation the MatLab Runtime is also installed thus it requires more space on the hard drive.

  • Name: CrySiV Standalone GUI
  • Recent version: 1.1
  • Developed for: Interactive crystallization simulation

Zoltan K. Nagy

Prof. Nagy joined Purdue University in 2012 as Professor of Chemical Engineering in the School of Chemical Engineering. Prior to this he was a Professor of Process Systems Engineering at Loughborough University, UK. His research interest includes pharmaceutical engineering, crystallization systems engineering, modelling, optimization and control of complex process systems, process analytical technologies, systems pedagogy and technology enhanced learning. He is associate editor for numerous international journals in the areas of process control and crystallization and received awards from IFAC, IEEE, IChemE, ACS, Royal Academy of Engineering, among others, for his work in advanced control, crystallization and his educational achievements.

  • Affiliation: Purdue University
  • Occupation: Education, Research and Development

Botond Szilagyi

Botond Szilagyi is PhD student at Babes-Bolyai University, having as co-supervisors prof. Z.K. Nagy and prof. B.G. Lakatos from University of Pannonia and Prof P.S. Agachi from the BBU. His research interest is the process engineering, especially the population balance based modelling, optimization and control of cooling crystallizers. He has more than ten ISI indexed publications and numerous conference presentations. He received awards and scholarships from EFCE, HCS (Hungarian Chemical Society), WFS (World Federation of Scientists) and Hungarian National Excellence Programme for the research results. He attended numerous research internships in Hungary, UK and US on the model based analysis of cooling crystallizers.
